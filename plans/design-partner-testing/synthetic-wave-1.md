# Synthetic Wave 1 Readout

## Status
This is a synthetic, non-proof research summary for the curated design-partner
testing program. It records a dry run with synthetic partner agents, not real
partners, partner data, production evidence, proof authority, SurfaceOps
decisions, JudgmentKit findings, or implementation claims.

The synthetic agents were prompted as blank-slate participants with no intended
prior project context. They received only synthetic organization context and a
short Surfaces Platform stimulus. Their responses are planning signals only.

## Dry-Run Method
The dry run used three synthetic partner archetypes:

- Northstar Group: a merger-integration enterprise that acquired three product
  companies with divergent UX, component libraries, terminology, and release
  practices.
- AtlasWorks: a large enterprise with 25+ internal and customer-facing tools,
  uneven design-system adoption, and increasing AI-assisted development.
- LumenHouse: a multi-brand platform company with seven consumer brands,
  shared frontend infrastructure, and brand-specific expression needs.

Each synthetic partner received the same Surfaces stimulus:

- Surfaces Platform is a proof-contract system for governed generated UI.
- The current loop is governed catalog authority, deterministic diagnostics,
  review-required non-promotion, and inert downstream proof outputs.
- Demos are presentation only, not proof.
- Current scope is proof-only and does not include live ingestion, full
  design-system support, live SurfaceOps, live JudgmentKit, production
  adapters, A2UI, SDKs, APIs, or live runtimes.

Each synthetic partner was asked to report:

- executive reaction;
- evidence-loop comprehension;
- workflow fit across generation, CI, review, and downstream consumption;
- acceptance signals;
- blockers;
- most important future proof target;
- pitch correction;
- Wave 2 decision.

## Executive Summary
Synthetic Wave 1 produced a strong positive signal for the governance/evidence
loop and a clear next-proof direction.

All three synthetic partners reported `Strong` evidence-loop comprehension with
`High` confidence. All three recommended continuing to Wave 2. The consistent
value signal was that Surfaces reads as a trust, governance, and CI layer for
generated UI rather than a UI generator.

The consistent blocker was also clear: the current two-component bounded
ingestion proof is too narrow for customer-scale relevance. Synthetic partners
want broader real-source ingestion, accessibility and behavior proof, governance
ownership clarity, CI conformance, and a credible path toward production-facing
adapter or runtime support.

## Recurring Acceptance Signals
The recurring acceptance signals were:

- explicit catalog authority and traceability;
- deterministic diagnostics tied to governed source material;
- review-required work cannot promote itself;
- evidence artifacts are inspectable by design-system, platform, governance,
  and executive stakeholders;
- proof artifacts are distinguishable from demos and production capability;
- CI-style gates are a natural adoption point.

## Recurring Blockers
| Severity | Blocker | Pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Current source ingestion is too narrow | All three partners said two components are not enough to assess portfolio, enterprise-suite, or multi-brand relevance. |
| High | No live Figma, Storybook, docs, or multi-source ingestion | All three partners said their actual source authority lives outside the current local proof bundle. |
| High | No production CI, API, SDK, adapter, or runtime path | AtlasWorks and LumenHouse explicitly need operational integration before adoption; Northstar also needs a path beyond static evidence. |
| Medium | Governance ownership is not yet clear | Northstar and LumenHouse need to know who resolves diagnostics, review-required states, and brand/platform conflicts. |
| Medium | Accessibility, behavior, density, terminology, and brand policy need stronger proof | All three partners asked for proof beyond visual component metadata. |
| Low | Demo/product confusion remains possible | Partners understood demos as presentation only, but warned that broader stakeholders may misread them. |

## Pitch Correction
Avoid broad language such as:

```text
Surfaces governs generated production UI.
```

Use tighter proof-first language:

```text
Surfaces proves whether generated UI conforms to declared design-system
authority before promotion.
```

For production-scope conversations, add:

```text
Current outputs are proof artifacts, not production APIs, SDKs, or runtimes.
```

## Wave 2 Decision
Proceed to synthetic Wave 2.

Synthetic Wave 2 should be harder and should focus on:

- ingestion scale and multi-source authority;
- accessibility, behavior, terminology, and brand-governance policy;
- CI conformance and promotion gates;
- exception workflows and ownership;
- production adapter or runtime path without overclaiming current proof scope.
